The induced dipole moment in a diamagnetic substance is always opposite to the

(c)
Bismuth is a diamagnetic substance. Hence, a toroid with a bismuth core has a magnetic

(d)
The permeability of ferromagnetic materials is not independent of the applied magnetic
field. It is greater for a lower field and vice versa.
(e)
The permeability of a ferromagnetic material is not less than one. It is always greater
than one. Hence, magnetic field lines are always nearly normal to the surface of such
materials at every point.

The maximum possible magnetisation of a paramagnetic sample can be of the same order
of magnitude as the magnetisation of a ferromagnet. This requires high magnetising fields
for saturation.

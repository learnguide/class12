Ans.(a)
The initial velocity of the particle is either parallel or anti-parallel to the magnetic
field. Hence, it travels along a straight path without suffering any deflection in the field.

Yes, the final speed of the charged particle will be equal to its initial speed. This is
because magnetic force can change the direction of velocity, but not its magnitude.

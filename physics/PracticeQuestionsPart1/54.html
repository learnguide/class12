7. Two charges
are placed at points A and B 6 cm apart.
1. Identify an equipotential surface of the system.
2. What is the direction of the electric field at every point on this surface?
Ans.
The situation is represented in the given figure.
An equipotential surface is the plane on which total potential is zero everywhere. This plane
is normal to line AB. The plane is located at the mid-point of line AB because the magnitude
of charges is the same.
1. The direction of the electric field at every point on this surface is normal to the plane in
the direction of AB.

Solution:
According to Maxwell’s theory accelerated charges radiate electromagnetic waves. Consider a charge
oscillating with some frequency. (An oscillating charge is an example of accelerating charge.) This produces
an oscillating electric field in space, which produces an oscillating magnetic field, which in turn, is a source
of oscillating electric field, and so on. The oscillating electric and magnetic fields thus regenerate each other,
so to speak, as the wave propagates through the space. The frequency of the electromagnetic wave naturally
equals the frequency of oscillation of the charge. The energy associated with the propagating wave comes at
the expense of the energy of the source
–
the accelerated charge.

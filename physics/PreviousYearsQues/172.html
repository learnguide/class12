(i) Why is the current under reverse bias almost independent of the applied potential upto a critical voltage ?
(ii) Why does the reverse current show a sudden increase at the critical voltage
Name any semiconductor device which operates under the reverse bias in the breakdown region.
Solution:
(i) When p-n junction is reverse baised, the majority carriers in p and n region are repelled away from the
junction. There is small current due to the minority carriers. This current attains its maximum or
saturation
value immediately and is independent of the applied reverse voltage
(ii) As the reverse voltage is increased to a certain value, called break down voltage, large amount of
covalent bonds in p and n regions are broken. As a result of this, large electron-hole pairs are
produced
which diffuse through the junction and hence there is a sudden rise in the reverse current. Once
break
down
voltage is reached, the high reverse current may damage the ordinary junction diode.

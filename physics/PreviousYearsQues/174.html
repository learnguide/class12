22. (a) What is linearly polarized light ? Describe briefly using a diagram how sunlight is polarised.
(b) Unpolarised light is incident on a polaroid. How would the intensity of transmitted light change when the
polaroid is rotated ?
Solution:
(a) When vibrations of light wave are confined to only one direction then light is called linearly polarised.
When sunlight passes through polaroid then components parallel to axis passes in unaffected way and
components perpendicular to axis are absorbed so transmitted light is polarised.
(b) On rotating the polaroid, intensity remains unchanged as half of the incident intensity.

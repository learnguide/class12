(c)
At atmospheric pressure, the ions of gases have no chance of reaching their respective
electrons because of collision and recombination with other gas molecules. Hence, gases are
insulators at atmospheric pressure. At low pressures, ions have a chance of reaching their
respective electrodes and constitute a current. Hence, they conduct electricity at these
pressures.
(d)
The work function of a metal is the minimum energy required for a conduction electron
to get out of the metal surface. All the electrons in an atom do not have the same energy
level. When a ray having some photon energy is incident on a metal surface, the electrons
come out from different levels with different energies. Hence, these emitted electrons show
different energy distributions.
